# Section 03 — Putaway (Allocation to Stock)

> Move received license plates into storage and make the stock available. **Channel:** PWA + ERP — putaway works by scan or by manual ERP entry (full fallback). See `00_Project_Overview.md`.

## Purpose
Take license plates staged in the wait/put zone (status "to putaway") and store them in a physical location, confirming by scan or manual entry. Only after putaway is confirmed does the stock become **available** for outbound and reporting.

## In Scope (v1)
- **Putaway task list** — one task per LPN awaiting putaway in the wait/put zone, for the operator's site (list ↔ detail).
- **Mixed-pallet decomposition** — a **mixed pallet** (`PLT-…`, one handling unit carrying many product/lot lines, built at receive — Section 02) is listed alongside single LPNs; selecting it walks each line through the **same** directed slotting + capacity/segregation/frozen guards, minting **one child LPN per line** (`decomposePalletLine`) and closing the pallet when its last line is placed. Transient parent → persistent homogeneous children, so stock stays one-product-per-LPN. **Both channels.**
  - **Operator chooses the decomposition order** — lines are **not** forced into a fixed sequence. The pallet manifest shows every line with its quantity; the operator **taps the product they want to place next** (e.g. the bulky items in front of them first), in any order. A partly-placed line keeps its remainder open for the next bin. ERP = clickable manifest chips; PWA = tappable manifest rows.
- **Putaway history (Completed)** — finished putaways do **not** vanish; the screen mirrors the Receive section's tabbed structure (**To put away** / **Completed**). The Completed tab is driven by the transaction log (`type:'putaway'`) and shows what was placed, where (from → to), by whom and when, with **label reprint** (re-prints from the record — never re-mints). **Both channels** (ERP table with reprint action; PWA list → `pwa-gr-lpn.html` label).
- **In-progress / partial-progress visibility** — a plate or pallet you have only *partly* worked is clearly marked and **resumable** with full read-only context (so an operator who paused mid-task sees what's already done on return):
  - **Single LPN split across bins:** a plate where some quantity is already placed shows a **"partial"** badge + **"X of Y placed"** in the worklist (action reads **Resume ›**); its detail carries a read-only **"Partially put away"** panel — placed/original with a progress bar, the remaining quantity, and each prior placement (qty → bin · child LPN · when · by). Driven by `lpnPutawayProgress(lpnId)` (a still-open source LPN's partial children link back to it via the txn `ref`).
  - **Mixed pallet partly decomposed:** a pallet with some lines placed shows a **"partial"** badge + **"X of N product line(s) placed"** (**Resume ›**); its decompose view shows a read-only **"Partially decomposed"** history (each placed line → bin · child LPN · when) and, inline on the manifest, **placed ✓ → bin** per done line and **"Z left · Y placed"** on a partly-placed line. Driven by `putawaysFromSource(palletId)`.
- **Directed putaway (suggested + override)** — the system ranks valid destinations and **pre-selects the recommended** one; the operator confirms it, taps another suggestion, or **assigns manually** (scan a label, or filter by Storage Area and pick a bin). Confirm by scan or manual ERP entry.
- **Ranking rule order:** product **home** (preferred location/area) → **consolidate** (same product + client already stored) → **Area category affinity** → open fallback.
- **Hard filters per candidate** (must pass to be offered): same site, active, **capacity OK** (weight + units + LPN/pallet slots), and **client–area segregation** (when ON). Excluded bins are listed with a **reason** (full / reserved).
- **Capacity & segregation enforcement** (pulled into v1): an LPN's weight = qty × base-unit weight; lot mixing in a bin allowed by default.
- **Partial putaway / split across bins** — when a plate (or pallet line) doesn't fit one bin, the operator enters a **quantity to place**; placing less than the full amount stores that quantity and **splits the remainder into a child LPN** (the source stays *to-putaway*) so it can go into another bin. Loops until the plate is fully stored. Conserves quantity (`Σ placed = original`) and carries lot/expiry/serial onto each child (`putawayPlace` / `decomposePalletLine` with a partial qty). Capacity/segregation/frozen are re-checked for the **entered** quantity, so a bin that fits the partial is offered even when it can't hold the whole plate.
- Suggestions show **why** (reason tags) and **headroom** (e.g. "Fits — 360/600 units · 198 kg · 2/6 slots").
- **Damage found at putaway / partial reject** — if the operator discovers damaged or suspect units while slotting a plate (**after** the inspection gate, or on good stock that skipped inspection), they can **reject a sub-quantity to a blocked disposition** (quarantine / hold / damaged) **before** storing the rest. The rejected units split onto a child LPN at the QA/quarantine location (a *hold* stays in place), **excluded from available stock**; the good remainder stays *to-putaway* and continues into a bin. Reason is **mandatory**, pulled from **Master Data › Reason Codes** (filtered by disposition). Conserves quantity + carries genealogy (`putawayReject`). **Both channels; single LPN** (mixed-pallet-line reject deferred).
- On confirm, stock becomes **available** at that location.

## Out of Scope (v2 backlog)
Cube/volume capacity, replenishment (bulk → pick face). *(LPN splitting across bins is now in v1 — see Partial putaway above.)*

## Key Concepts / Entities
- **Putaway task** — pending storage action for an LPN.
- **LPN** — the handling unit being stored (carries lot/expiry/serial from receipt).
- **Mixed pallet (`PLT-…`)** — a transient aggregate handling unit (many product/lot lines under one plate) that **decomposes** at putaway into one child LPN per line, then closes.
- **Location** — the destination bin.
- **Stock record** — becomes "available" at the location after confirm.

## Process Flow
1. PWA/ERP lists pending putaway tasks (LPNs in the wait/put zone) for the operator's site.
2. Operator selects an LPN (scan or pick from list).
3. System ranks valid destinations (**home → consolidate → category → open**), applies hard filters (site, active, capacity, segregation), and **pre-selects the recommended** one. Excluded bins are shown with a reason.
4. Operator sets the **quantity to place** (default = the whole plate), then confirms the recommendation, taps a different suggestion, or **assigns manually** (scan a label, or filter by Storage Area and choose a bin).
5. System validates the chosen location for the entered quantity (same site, active, capacity, segregation) → OK / warning / error.
6. Confirm → that quantity is stored at the location and flips to **available**; any remainder splits into a child LPN that stays *to-putaway* for the next bin. Written to transaction history.

## Business Rules
- Destination must belong to the **same site** as the LPN and be **active**.
- **Capacity** (weight + units + LPN/pallet slots) is enforced at putaway; an over-capacity bin is not offered and is rejected on manual entry. Unset limits = unlimited.
- **Frozen scope is refused:** a bin inside an active physical-inventory **`frozen`** stock-take is excluded from suggestions (shown "frozen — PHY-…") and rejected on manual entry until the take is posted or abandoned (`isLocFrozen` / `frozenTakeFor`, shared with Move / Transfer-ship / Allocation — Section 05).
- **Client–area segregation:** when ON, only the client's own Areas + unowned (shared) Areas are valid; others are blocked. When OFF, Areas are shared.
- Ranking is **home → consolidate → category → open**; the recommended destination is pre-selected, but the operator may override with any valid location.
- One plate may be **split across multiple bins** (partial putaway): the entered quantity is stored and the remainder mints a child LPN that stays *to-putaway* for the next bin (conservation `Σ placed = original`; genealogy carried). Whole-plate placement (the default) keeps the plate's own id. Lot mixing in a bin allowed by default. **A partial child's putaway txn records `ref` = the source LPN id** (a whole placement records `ref:'putaway'`), so the source's partial progress (placed/remaining + where each part went) is reconstructable and shown read-only until the plate is fully stored.
- **Mixed pallets decompose** into one child LPN per line; a line may itself be **placed across bins** (partial). Each placement re-asserts the shared capacity/segregation/frozen guards at commit, mints the child via `decomposePalletLine`, and the pallet closes when its last line is fully placed. **Lines may be placed in any operator-chosen order** — there is no forced sequence.
- **Damage discovered at putaway is peeled off, not stored:** the operator may reject a sub-quantity (or the whole plate) to a blocked disposition — the rejected qty becomes a child LPN at the QA/quarantine location (a *hold* stays in place), **excluded from availability**, while the good remainder stays *to-putaway*. It is logged as a **stock-status-change** txn (`type:'status'`) so it lands in the audit trail / txn report — **not** a `putaway` txn, so it never appears as a completed putaway. Reason mandatory, master-data driven per disposition (`putawayReject`). *(Stock Status — Section 05 — deliberately won't touch a `to-putaway` plate, so this is the in-flow path for that case; the alternative — store it then re-disposition — would briefly make damaged stock available.)*
- Stock is not available until putaway is confirmed; the LPN retains its lot/expiry/serial genealogy.
- Putaway is available on both PWA (scan) and ERP (manual entry); every putaway is written to the transaction history (audit) and surfaced on the **Completed** tab, where its label can be **reprinted** (from the record, never re-minted).

## Screens (for mockup phase)
- **PWA:** putaway task list with **To do / Completed** tabs. *To do* (single LPNs **and** mixed pallets): scan-LPN/pallet → **quantity-to-place** field (default = whole; less splits across bins) → ranked suggestions (recommended pre-selected) + "assign manually" → scan/confirm location; a scanned pallet **decomposes** line-by-line — the **manifest rows are tappable**, so lines are placed **in any order** (each line itself splittable). Part-worked plates/pallets carry a **"partial"** badge + **"X/Y placed"** in the list, a read-only **"Partially put away"** card in the LPN detail (placed/remaining + each placement), and inline **placed ✓ → bin** / **"Z left · Y placed"** on the pallet manifest. The single-LPN detail also carries a **"⚠ Report damage / reject"** control (reject qty → disposition → master-data reason) that peels damaged units off to a blocked disposition before the good remainder is stored. *Completed*: recent putaways, tap a row to **reprint** its label.
- **ERP:** putaway task list with **To put away / Completed** tabs. *To put away* = detail with a **quantity-to-place** field (splits the remainder across bins), **selectable suggestion cards**, an **"Assign manually"** toggle (scan or Area-filtered bin pick), a single **Selected destination** panel carrying the one Confirm, an **excluded-with-reason** list, and a **segregation** toggle. A separate **"Mixed pallets to decompose"** worklist drives pallet decomposition through the same picker; the manifest **chips are clickable** to place lines **in any order**. Part-worked plates/pallets show a **"partial"** badge + **"X of Y placed"** and a **Resume ›** action; the detail carries a read-only **"Partially put away / decomposed"** progress panel (bar + each prior placement: qty → bin · child LPN · when · by) and a collapsible **"Found damage? — ⚠ Report damage / reject"** panel (reject qty → disposition → master-data reason) for peeling damaged units off to a blocked disposition before storing the good remainder. *Completed* = a putaway-history table (when · LPN · client · product · lot/expiry · qty · from → to · by · source) with a **Reprint label** action. Full manual fallback.

## Dependencies
Consumes LPNs from Section 02 and, from Section 01, **Storage Areas, location capacity, product category & preferred storage, and the client–area segregation setting**. Makes stock available for Section 04 (Stock-Out) and Section 06 (Reports).
